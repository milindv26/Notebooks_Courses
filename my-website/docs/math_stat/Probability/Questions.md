# Questions

1. Three distinct numbers are selected from 100 natural numbers. What is the probability that all the three numbers are divisible by 2 and 3?
2. A divisor of 2700 is selected. What is the probability that it is a multiple of 4 but not 5?
3. Robot is 3 cm from the left of the table and 17cm from the right of the table. The robot moves with equal probability 10cm to the left or to the right. What is the average number of steps for the robot to fall of off the table?
4. You and your friend are playing a game with a fair coin. The two of you will continue to toss the coin until the sequence HH or TH shows up. If HH shows up first, you win, and if TH shows up first your friend win. What is the probability of you winning the game?
5. The letters of the word 'ASSASSIN' are written down at random in a row. What is the probability that no two 'S' occur together?
6. Out of 21 tickets market with numbers from 1 to 21, three are drawn at random. What is the probability that the numbers form an Arithmetic Progression?
7. Three distinct numbers are selected from first 30 natural numbers. What is the probability that their sum is divisible by 3?
8. Twelve coins are divided into four groups of three coins each. The coins in each group have probabilities of landing heads equal to  $p=\dfrac{1}{2},\dfrac{1}{3},\dfrac{1}{5},\dfrac{1}{9}$, respectively. All coins are tossed independently. Find the probability that the total number of heads obtained is odd.
9. How many distinct paths are there from the grid point (0,0) to the grid point (6,6) on a 6×6 lattice if you can only move to adjacent grid points by going one unit right or one unit up?
10. On average, how many cards in a normal deck of 52 playing cards do you need to flip over to observe your first ace?
